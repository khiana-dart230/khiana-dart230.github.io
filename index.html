The Little Fox

In a den within the hills,

The sun shines bright upon the slumbering,

Where a fox awakens, groggy yet still.

The fox, so little and eager for the day

Finds it is motherless, left with only footprints in the dirt.

So the fox wandered curious as to where she went away.


There the fox trots, on and on for a while,

Down a small, narrow path the little fox has been down once,

Soon the bug filled path lead to a river, it came across its mother with a smile.

For you see, she had simply went to drink some water.

So the little fox joined in, as the journey was a bit of a bother.
